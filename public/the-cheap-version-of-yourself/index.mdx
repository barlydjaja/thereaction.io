It was a regular sunday morning, the rain was pouring quite heavily. I woke up at around 10am that day.
I got up, open my laptop, and started to work on my side hustle.

There it was, a 2 hour vibe coding session. Create a full interactive [Drive Folio](https://barlydjaja.com) from scratch.

Did i write a single line of code? Nope. The AI did all the work.

Did i have the adequate knowledge to utilize three.js? Nope. I only took insipration of [Bruno Simon's](https://bruno-simon.com)

I solved a bug that the team didn't realize, how did i fix it? It was because of AI, a bigger model, a smarter model, a more expensive model

I was once proud of my thinking and analyzing process, i was once proud of the quality i was able to deliver, i was once proud of who i was. Who i was becoming

But now, I'm not even sure if i could still deliver the same quality, i'm not even sure if i'm still the same person
I'm not even sure if i could still grow as i was growing.

So let's be real here, what does AI actually gave you?
For me, it was speed, as long as you know how to prompt it, anything can be delivered with almost the same quality.
But what did it take from me?

> Everything

The person who was becoming, the person who was proud of his art, the person who want to grow his wings.
But all of this is not possible anymore, because my art is starting to become irrelevant, i'm starting to not enjoy the process anymore.
The company now requires engineer to work 10x faster, delivering 10x more features. Whether we want it or not, we must use AI.
Even my own brain doesn't want to think as hard as before, I just want to finish my work and lay in bed, play games, doom scrolling the internet

The output is the same, it's almost impossible to tell whether it was written by human or AI.
The output was never the problem, the output is just a byproduct of you. But it will never makes me grow

Most AI discussion i see nowadays are just a bunch of code slop and bugs created in the system. But no one talks
about it's real impacts. The residues that us humans are created of. I believe we are a residue of our past self.
We are exactly what we were, a fraction of our past, the struggles that we went through, the struggle that doesn't kills us makes us stronger
But in the world of AI, all of this residues are no more. We are not made up of residues anymore, we got the finished products in seconds.

Adulthood is a residue of our past struggles, wins, and losses. Without those experience will not make us who we are today. We learn and we grow through struggles.
But what happens when all this struggles are gone? are we even the same person anymore?

Other arguments are saying, isn't AI just a tool? just like everyother tools, calculator, smartphone, google, wikipedia, etc?
No. Those tools removes the labor of works, not the practice of thoughts itself. AI practically removes the process of struggle, core essence of creating an idea is through a struggle. Through a deadend.
That's not labor. That's where we grow, our residues that makes us, us.

So you think, it's only matter of time, before we adapt and become better version of ourselves, creating new things with AI.
But that's where the illusion kicks in. Did you ever realize that, AI is becoming more and more better
And we are becoming more and more lazy? I surely do feel so, How could i not? I used to code for 8 hours a day, now i just code for 2 hours and the rest of the time i'm just
waiting for AI to finish my work. Or just wait for the limit to reset, so i can use the premium models again.

If an AI could do this for you and you'd be content with the result, you're probably not doing it for the result

So what am i becoming? what am i doing to prevent this? Should it even be prevented?
I don't know, and I'm scared.
I wrote this blog, using my own words, rewrote it a couple of times, Even still i don't have the confidence to release it without consulting to an LLM.

I just hope i don't become a cheap version of myself.

Do you?
